Few months back i had a PR rejected for the dumbest reason....

i had written some labels directly in the code. button text, headings, small messages. normal stuff. it worked perfectly.

my team lead **Jagan Kumar Mudila** asked me to pull all of them out into a constants file 🤷‍♂️

i genuinely thought it was a waste of time. the text was right there. why move it somewhere else and import it back.... it felt like extra work for zero benefit.

i pushed back a little. he didnt force it as a rule, he just said "you'll see why later".

i did it anyway, half annoyed 🥲

<Pull>Then months later a school asked us for the product in their regional language.</Pull>

and suddenly that "waste of time" was the only reason it wasnt a nightmare.

all the text was in one place. one file. we could swap languages without hunting through 40 components praying we didnt miss one.

<Ex>Ex: imagine finding every hardcoded "Submit" "Save" "Welcome" across the whole app under deadline.... no thanks 😮‍💨</Ex>

that i when i understood what he actually meant.

the constants thing was never about today. it was about the change that hadnt happened yet.

good engineering isnt optimising for the task in front of you. its leaving the door open for the thing you cant see coming.

i learned that not from a course.... i learned it by being wrong first.
