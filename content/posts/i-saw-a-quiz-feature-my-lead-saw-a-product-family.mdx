when the principal gave us the quiz idea, my brain went straight to building.

screens. scoring. timers. sync. ship it, done 🤷‍♂️

i was thinking of it as a feature.

**Jagan** sir was thinking of it as a company.

he literally thought like zoho.... one organization, many products under it.

<Ex>
Ex:<br />
maahita.com → the organization<br />
school.maahita.com → the school ERP<br />
new quiz idea → give it a name → vinodam.maahita.com<br />
<br />
one tree, many branches.
</Ex>

so the quiz didnt stay "the quiz thing we built for that school".

it got a name. **vinodam**.

it got a home. its own subdomain under the org.

and the branding.... we didnt even need a new logo. we already had the māhita banyan tree. kids playing around the tree → thats vinodam 🌳

a separate product, but clearly part of the same family. you see it once and you know its ours.

*the māhita tree → vinodam: kids playing around the same tree*

then came the part i really didnt expect. the marketing.

we didnt pitch it to the principal as "a quiz tool".

we pitched it as an app for children engagement in summer holidays.... kids learn by attending quizzes for fun. learning, packaged as play.

same software. completely different story. and THAT story is what the principal bought.

<Pull>a feature solves today's request. a product gets a name, a brand, and a future.</Pull>

i was thinking like a developer. build what was asked.

he was thinking like a company. name it, brand it, position it, sell it.

that day i understood why founders see differently. the code was maybe 70% of the work. the thinking around it is what made it a product.

the app → [Vinodam](https://vinodam-maahita.web.app/)
