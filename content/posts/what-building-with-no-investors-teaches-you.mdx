our company has no investors. three founders put their own salaries into the idea.

i didnt get what that meant until i'd been here a while.

it means every feature has a real cost that someone in the room is personally paying. server bills, time, opportunity. nothing is monopoly money.

so the questions change.

its never "would this be cool to build". its "will a school actually use this, and will it help them pay us" ....

<Pull>When the money is real, every decision becomes a product decision.</Pull>

i've sat in calls where we said no to features that sounded great. not because they were bad ideas. because we couldnt afford to be wrong about them.

<Ex>Ex: a fancy analytics dashboard vs fixing the fee-receipt flow schools complain about weekly. one is impressive. the other gets renewals.</Ex>

this also changed how i write code. i think about maintenance cost now. who fixes this in 6 months. what happens when this breaks during school hours.

big companies can absorb waste. we cant. so i learned to feel the cost of things early.

honestly i think every engineer should work at least once in a place where the money is real 🙏
