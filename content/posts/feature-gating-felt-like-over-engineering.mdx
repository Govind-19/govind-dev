early on we were building features for exactly one kind of user. every school got everything.

then in planning someone said we need feature gating. plans, limits, which school gets which module....

i remember thinking why. we dont even have paying customers yet 😅

it felt like building a toll booth on a road nobody drives on.

but the seniors had seen this before. they made us wire the checks in early. every feature went behind a flag tied to the school's plan. boring plumbing work. no demo value. nothing to show anyone.

<Pull>Then our first school actually agreed to pay. And they wanted a smaller plan than what we'd been giving away free.</Pull>

suddenly we needed to turn things OFF for one customer without breaking everyone else.

because the gating already existed it was a config change. one day of work, mostly testing.

without it.... we'd have been ripping if-conditions into 30 screens while a paying customer waited. our first ever revenue, stuck behind a rewrite.

<Ex>Ex: "can we hide the transport module for this school by Monday" is a very different question when every screen already asks permission first.</Ex>

the lesson wasnt about subscriptions. its that the business changes faster than the code wants to.

the boring plumbing is what lets you say yes when it matters.
