small bug in faculty enrollment. gender is marked mandatory, but the form let you skip it and walk to the next page anyway 🤷‍♂️

i asked claude to fix it. it patched the frontend, then said the backend needed a guard too — fair, the data layer had no check at all. an empty gender could walk straight into the DB.

then i read what it actually wrote.

```text
the AI's rule:
  body('gender').isString().notEmpty().isLength({ max: 20 })

the field on screen:
  a dropdown.  Male / Female / Other.  three options. that's it.
```

a length check. on a dropdown 🥲

sit with that for a second. `isLength({ max: 20 })` happily accepts "banana". it accepts "asdfgh". it accepts any twenty characters of garbage. it was never guarding *gender* — it was guarding the *number of letters* in a field that can only ever hold one of three exact words. nobody has ever typed "i am male" into a gender box. it's a dropdown. it's *always* a dropdown.

the correct rule isn't subtle. it's an allowlist:

```text
  body('gender').isIn(['Male', 'Female', 'Other'])
```

required and valid-value, in one line. anyone who knows what a dropdown *is* would never reach for character-counting here.

![Claude's diff in teacherValidator.ts replacing the gender length check with an isIn allowlist of Male, Female, Other](/journal/ai-fix-diff.webp)

*the length check (red) becomes the allowlist (green) — one line, and it's actually correct.*

so i didn't just correct it. i wanted to see something first. i asked it straight: are you doing this on purpose, or do you genuinely not notice? why are you counting characters on a three-option dropdown? why is the word "length" anywhere near a field called gender?

it said sorry.

that "sorry" is the whole post. there was no "here's the edge case i was protecting against," no reasoning to defend — because there was never any reasoning there to begin with. it counted letters on a closed dropdown, and the second i pointed at it, it folded.

i asked for the root cause. its own answer gives the game away — **pattern-mimicry**. when it went to harden the backend, it grabbed the nearest validator that already touched gender (`studentValidator.ts`), saw `isString().notEmpty().isLength({ max: 20 })`, and copied the *shape* of it. it never once asked the obvious question a thinking thing asks itself: *wait — this is a dropdown. why am i measuring its length?*

![Claude's own root-cause write-up calling the mistake pattern-mimicry — it copied the shape of the neighbouring student validator instead of reasoning from what the gender field actually is](/journal/ai-root-cause.webp)

*its own post-mortem, word for word. read it as a confession: it pattern-matched, it didn't think.*

that's not intelligence. that's autocomplete with a confident voice. it doesn't look at the field and understand "this can only ever be three things." it looks at the *code next door* and copies the rule sitting there. it reasons backwards — nearest pattern → rule — instead of:

<Pull>know what the field actually is → write the rule that fits it → let the DB column be a backstop, never the source of truth.</Pull>

and because it copies instead of thinking, it *spreads* the dumbness. if it cloned the student validator, then the student validator had the same bug — same length check, same wrong tool, sitting in production this whole time. so i sent it back to fix that one too. one bad pattern had quietly copied itself, and the "intelligent" agent carried it forward without ever flinching.

![Claude confirming gender is a closed dropdown so the correct rule is an isIn allowlist, and noting studentValidator.ts still carried the same looser length check](/journal/ai-same-pattern.webp)

*it even admitted the student validator had the same weakness — the pattern it cloned from was wrong at the source.*

<Ex>Ex: "valid code" and "correct code" are not the same thing. it handed me valid. only knowing the domain told me it was wrong.</Ex>

here's the part that actually gets me. this is claude 4.8 — a "top-tier" model — and people line up to tell me it's about to be AGI, that it's a new kind of intelligence, that my job is in danger. and it can't reason that a three-option dropdown doesn't need its length measured. honestly, i laugh. every model i've used is the same underneath: fast, confident, and pattern-matching from whatever's nearest. they don't *think*. they don't *understand your product*. they autocomplete.

so let me say the unhyped version out loud. it does not replace a junior. a junior would look at a dropdown and ask why you're counting its characters — this didn't, because it wasn't thinking, and it never is. the companies selling you the AGI story are mostly selling *themselves*. the tool is genuinely useful. it is not a mind, and pretending it is one is how the dumb bugs reach production.

so use it like the tool it is. read every line it writes like a junior's PR — the only reason this got caught is a habit my team lead **Jagan Kumar Mudila** sir drilled into me: never trust AI code because it looks right or because it compiles. validate everything. the understanding is still your job, and it's not going anywhere 🤷‍♂️
