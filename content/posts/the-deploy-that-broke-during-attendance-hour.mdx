there's a specific feeling when production breaks and you know real people are staring at a spinner because of you.

we pushed a deploy in the morning. small change, looked harmless. tested on our side.

what we didnt think about.... morning is attendance hour. teachers across schools were marking attendance at that exact moment.

the deploy broke it. calls started coming.

my first instinct was to panic and start changing things fast. the seniors did the opposite. slow down. reproduce it. find the actual cause before touching anything.

<Pull>Fixing the wrong thing fast is worse than fixing the right thing slow.</Pull>

we found the root cause, rolled out the fix, and schools were back before the period ended.

but the real change came after. we sat and asked how this got out at all.

<Ex>Ex: our checklist now literally asks "what time is it for the schools" before any deploy. attendance hour is frozen. no exceptions.</Ex>

things that changed forever after that day....

deploys moved out of school working hours. releases got a proper checklist instead of vibes. rollback steps written down BEFORE deploying, not invented during the fire.

no tutorial teaches this because no tutorial has a teacher standing in front of 40 kids waiting for your fix 😮‍💨

production is a different teacher. it grades hard and it doesnt repeat the lesson.
